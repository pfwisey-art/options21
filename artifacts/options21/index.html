The tokenisation
revolution

Tokenisation — representing real-world assets on a blockchain as digital tokens — is rapidly moving from experiment to reality. Major banks, asset managers and regulators are actively building the infrastructure for a tokenised financial market.

For retail investors, this represents a genuine broadening of opportunity: access to asset classes, diversification strategies and yield sources that were previously out of reach. But it also demands a higher level of due diligence and risk awareness than conventional investing.

Tokenisable asset classes
Asset Class Min. (Tokenised) Status
Real Estate ~$50 ● Live
Government Bonds ~$1,000 ● Live
Private Equity ~$100 ◑ Emerging
Infrastructure ~$500 ◑ Emerging
Fine Art / Collectibles ~$10 ○ Early Stage
Read the full trade note →
🔗 Fractional Ownership

A $10M office building divided into 10M tokens worth $1 each. Retail investors buy $100 worth — receiving proportional rental income and capital growth. Previously impossible without significant capital.

⚡ T+0 Settlement

Blockchain-based settlement compresses the traditional two-day (T+2) clearing cycle to seconds — reducing capital tied up as collateral and systemic risk across markets.

🌐 24/7 Global Markets

A Sydney investor can buy a fraction of a London property at 2am Sunday and trade out within minutes. Tokenised markets operate continuously with no geographic restriction.

⚠️ Key Risks to Understand
✗ Regulatory uncertainty across jurisdictions
✗ Smart contract bugs — errors may be irreversible
✗ Liquidity illusion — thin secondary markets
✗ Tax treatment remains evolving and complex
